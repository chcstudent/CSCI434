What's a Tabletop RPG?

A tabletop RPG (roleplaying game) is a cooperative experience, where players collaborate to tell a story of adventure, danger, and intrigue. The most popular example is Dungeons and Dragons, but it would be unfair to the genre to say they're all the same, just like saying all novels or all movies are the same. There is as much nuance among tabletop RPGs as exists between book genres or film categories. But there are 3 main elements to every tabletop RPG: Storytelling, Setting, and Mechanics.
Storytelling refers to how the players tell the story. The most popular option is a Game Master. They serve as a narrator to the game's events, driving the plot and managing story elements such as setting and characters that aren't controlled by the other players. This is the method Shattered uses, but it's not the only option.
Setting is simply that: where the game takes place. Medieval Fantasy is a popular setting, often borrowing elements from European history and mythology. Your "knights in shining armor" and all that. Shattered is set in an entirely original world named Feneryss that is slowly recovering from a cataclysmic event. Its denizens face many struggles: the Undead Tide, eldritch abominations, manic weather patterns, and much more.
Mechanics refers to how players interact with the game elements. Many games use dice. Shattered is one of them. Many others, however, use a deck of cards. Some even have unique cards or dice sets that are specifically crafted for their mechanics, such as FATE. As stated, Shattered uses dice, and not just the dice most people know, with six sides. We use four-, six-, eight- and ten-sided dice together to represent a character's skill (or lack thereof) whenever they perform actions that have meaningful consequences for success and failure.
